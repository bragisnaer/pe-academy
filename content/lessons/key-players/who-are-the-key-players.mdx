The private equity ecosystem involves a range of participants — from the fund managers who source and execute deals, to the institutional investors who provide capital, to the advisers, lenders, and management teams who enable transactions.

At its core: PE firms act as GPs, institutional investors (pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, endowments, insurance companies, and family offices) act as LPs, and investment banks, law firms, and consultancies support deal execution on both the buy and sell side.

This lesson maps the full ecosystem of PE participants, explains each player's incentives and role in the deal lifecycle, and shows how the relationships between GPs, LPs, portfolio companies, and EBITDA-focused advisers create the conditions for value creation — or destruction.
