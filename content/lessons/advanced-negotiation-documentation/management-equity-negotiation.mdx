### The Core Tension

The PE fund wants management fully aligned through equity participation. Management wants maximum upside with minimum personal financial risk. The negotiation centers on:

**Investment amount.** How much personal capital must management invest? Typically 1–3 years of base salary. The amount must be meaningful (to demonstrate commitment) but not ruinous (if the investment is total loss, management should not face personal financial catastrophe).

**Equity allocation.** What percentage of the company's equity does management receive? In a typical mid-market buyout, management holds 10–20% of the equity, with the PE fund holding 80–90%.

**Ratchet / sweet equity.** Management's equity may include a ratchet mechanism: if the PE fund achieves a specified return threshold (e.g., 2.5x MOIC), management's equity percentage increases (e.g., from 15% to 25%). This gives management outsized upside above the threshold while protecting the fund's minimum return.

> **Market-specific note:** Ratchet structures are particularly common in European PE. In the US, stock options are more prevalent. Tax treatment drives the structural choice: in many European jurisdictions, shares acquired at entry have more favourable capital gains treatment than options exercised later. In the UK, Enterprise Management Incentive (EMI) options provide tax advantages for qualifying companies. Danish tax treatment of management equity depends on whether shares are acquired through employment (potentially taxed as income) or as an independent investment (capital gains treatment).

**Leaver provisions.** What happens to management's equity if they leave? Good leaver (fair value or above) vs. bad leaver (cost or below). The definition of "good leaver" and "bad leaver" is heavily negotiated.

**Tag-along and drag-along rights.** Tag-along: management can sell their shares alongside the PE fund at exit (on the same terms). Drag-along: the PE fund can compel management to sell their shares at exit (preventing a minority holdout that blocks a sale). Both are standard.
