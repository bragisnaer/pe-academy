Private equity refers to investment capital raised from institutional and accredited investors to acquire, invest in, or provide financing to private companies — those not listed on public stock exchanges.

Unlike public markets where anyone can buy shares, PE funds pool committed capital from limited partners (LPs) such as pension funds, endowments, and family offices, and deploy it through a concentrated portfolio of private company investments over a defined fund life.

This lesson introduces the core definition of private equity, how it differs from public market investing, and why the asset class has grown to manage trillions of dollars in assets globally.
