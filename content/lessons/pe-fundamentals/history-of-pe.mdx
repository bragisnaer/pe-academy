Private equity as an industry emerged in its modern form in the 1970s and 1980s, when firms like KKR pioneered the leveraged buyout (LBO) — using significant debt alongside equity to acquire companies, improve operations, and exit at a profit.

The 1980s saw explosive growth driven by high-yield bond markets and landmark transactions like the RJR Nabisco buyout, which at $25 billion remained the largest LBO in history for over two decades and was dramatised in the book "Barbarians at the Gate."

This lesson traces the evolution of private equity from early venture-backed startups through the LBO boom, the post-financial-crisis regulatory changes, and into the current era of mega-funds managing hundreds of billions in dry powder.
