PE firms operating across borders must navigate multiple regulatory frameworks that are evolving independently and sometimes in conflicting directions.

### Key Regulatory Dimensions

**Fund regulation.** The US (Dodd-Frank, SEC oversight), EU (AIFMD, AIFMD II), and UK (post-Brexit FCA regime) impose different registration, reporting, and marketing requirements. A PE firm marketing to US, EU, and UK investors must comply with three distinct regulatory frameworks simultaneously.

**Tax.** Transfer pricing rules, interest deductibility limitations (ATAD in the EU, Section 163(j) in the US), withholding taxes on cross-border payments, and capital gains tax treatment all vary by jurisdiction and interact in complex ways. The OECD's BEPS (Base Erosion and Profit Shifting) initiative has reduced aggressive tax structuring, but significant differences remain.

**Data privacy.** GDPR (EU), UK GDPR (UK post-Brexit), CCPA/CPRA (California), PIPL (China), and numerous other national data protection laws create a patchwork of compliance obligations for PE firms and their portfolio companies, particularly those operating cross-border digital businesses.

**ESG and sustainability.** The EU leads with SFDR (fund-level sustainability disclosure), CSRD (corporate sustainability reporting), and the EU Taxonomy (classification of sustainable activities). The US has been less prescriptive (though the SEC proposed climate disclosure rules). This divergence creates complexity for GPs managing global portfolios and reporting to LPs across jurisdictions.

**Antitrust and foreign investment.** Merger control thresholds, review timelines, and foreign investment screening criteria differ significantly across the US, EU, UK, China, India, and other jurisdictions. A single cross-border transaction may require filing in 5–10 jurisdictions, each with different thresholds, timelines, and review criteria.

### Practical Implications for PE Firms

- **Legal structuring** must account for regulatory requirements in every jurisdiction where the fund operates, markets, or invests
- **Compliance teams** have expanded significantly — regulatory compliance is now a material cost centre for PE firms
- **Fund terms** may need jurisdiction-specific provisions to accommodate different regulatory requirements
- **Portfolio companies** must maintain compliance across all jurisdictions where they operate — a growing burden as businesses globalise
