The relationship between a general partner (GP) and limited partners (LPs) is the cornerstone of how private equity funds operate — defining who controls investment decisions, who bears liability, and how economics are split.

GPs contribute a small amount of capital (typically 1–2% of the fund, known as GP commit) but earn a management fee (usually 2% of committed capital annually) and carried interest (typically 20% of profits above a hurdle rate), aligning their interests with LP returns.

This lesson examines the legal structure of the GP/LP agreement, the mechanics of capital calls and distributions, the preferred return and waterfall provisions, and how LPs monitor fund performance through quarterly reports and advisory committees (LPACs).
