A private equity fund is a closed-end vehicle with a typical lifespan of 10 years — a defined period during which capital is committed, deployed into investments, managed, and ultimately returned to investors through exits.

The fund structure separates the general partner (GP), who manages the fund and makes investment decisions, from the limited partners (LPs), who provide the bulk of the capital and receive the majority of returns but take no active role in management.

This lesson walks through the full fund lifecycle: fundraising and capital commitments, the investment period (typically years 1–5), the hold and value-creation period, and the exit and distribution phase — including how carried interest and the J-curve shape returns over time.
