The **Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA)** is the governing document of a PE fund. It defines every aspect of the GP-LP relationship: fees, carried interest, governance, investment restrictions, and reporting obligations. LPA negotiation is the battleground where GP and LP interests are balanced.

### Key Economic Terms

**Management fee:** Typically 1.5–2.0% of committed capital during the investment period, stepping down to 1.0–1.5% of invested capital thereafter. Larger funds and more competitive markets tend to produce lower management fees.

**Carried interest:** Standard is 20% of profits above the hurdle rate. Some GPs charge 25% or even 30% for outperforming funds (rare, and typically only accepted from the highest-performing managers). European LPs have pushed for "whole-fund" (European) carry waterfalls rather than deal-by-deal (American) waterfalls.

**Hurdle rate:** Typically 8% preferred return. Some recent funds have reduced this to 7% or even 6%, though 8% remains the market standard.

**GP commitment:** Standard is 1–2% of fund size, but large LPs increasingly push for 3–5% to ensure deeper GP alignment.

**Transaction and monitoring fees:** Historically, GPs charged transaction fees (paid by portfolio companies to the GP for advisory services at acquisition) and monitoring fees (ongoing annual fees for board participation). LP pressure has largely eliminated these in newer funds, with most LPAs now requiring 100% offset of such fees against management fees.

### Key Governance Terms

**Key-person clause:** If named senior investment professionals (typically 2–4 partners) depart or reduce their commitment to the firm, the investment period is suspended until the situation is resolved or LPs vote to reinstate it. This is the LP's most powerful governance tool.

**No-fault divorce (removal clause):** A supermajority of LPs (typically 75–80%) can vote to terminate the GP's management of the fund, with or without cause. This "nuclear option" is rarely exercised but provides an ultimate safeguard.

**Investment restrictions:** Limits on the maximum percentage of the fund that can be invested in a single deal (typically 15–25%), restrictions on investing outside the stated strategy (sector, geography, deal size), and limitations on fund-level leverage.

**Conflicts of interest:** Rules governing GP behaviour when conflicts arise between the fund and the GP's other activities (other funds, co-investments, personal investments).
