Fundraising is the lifeblood of a PE firm. Without LP capital, there are no deals. The fundraising process typically begins 12–18 months before the target first close and can last 6–18 months depending on market conditions and the GP's track record.

### The Fundraising Timeline

**Phase 1: Preparation (3–6 months).** The GP prepares marketing materials: the Private Placement Memorandum (PPM), track record data, team biographies, investment strategy overview, and due diligence questionnaire (DDQ) responses. Existing fund performance is audited and presented in a standardised format.

**Phase 2: Marketing (6–12 months).** The GP meets prospective LPs — typically 50–200 institutional investors — through roadshows, one-on-one meetings, and annual LP meetings. A **placement agent** (an intermediary that helps GPs access LP networks) may be engaged, particularly for first-time or smaller funds.

**Phase 3: First close.** Once sufficient commitments are gathered (typically 50–70% of the target fund size), the GP holds a first close and can begin making investments. Additional LPs are admitted at subsequent closes.

**Phase 4: Final close.** The fund reaches its target size (or hard cap, the maximum fund size). No further LP commitments are accepted.

### What LPs Evaluate

Institutional LPs conduct rigorous due diligence before committing to a fund:

- **Track record** — the GP's historical returns across prior funds (net IRR, net MOIC, DPI). Track record persistence (whether top-quartile funds consistently produce top-quartile successors) is scrutinised closely.
- **Team stability** — have the key investment professionals remained with the firm across fund generations? GP key-person risk is a top concern for LPs.
- **Investment strategy** — is the strategy clear, differentiated, and consistent? Has the GP demonstrated discipline in sticking to its stated approach?
- **Alignment of interests** — how much GP capital is committed? Are fee terms competitive? Is the carried interest structure fair?
- **Operational capabilities** — does the GP have a credible value creation playbook? Are operating partners or portfolio operations resources in place?
- **ESG integration** — how does the GP incorporate environmental, social, and governance considerations into its investment process? This has moved from "nice to have" to a threshold requirement for many European and increasingly US LPs.

### Fundraising in Different Market Environments

In strong markets with attractive GP track records, top-tier funds are oversubscribed — they raise more capital than planned and may turn away LPs. In difficult markets (2009, 2023), fundraising timelines lengthen, fund sizes may be cut, and GPs make fee concessions to attract capital.
