Understanding how a PE firm makes money as a business — distinct from how the fund makes money for LPs — is essential for understanding GP behaviour and incentives.

### GP Revenue Streams

**Management fees** provide steady, predictable revenue. For a $2 billion fund charging 2%, management fees generate $40 million annually — enough to cover a firm of 50–80 professionals with offices in major financial centres.

**Carried interest** provides outsized upside when funds perform well. For a $2 billion fund generating 2.5x net MOIC, total carry might reach $200–300 million (20% of $1–1.5 billion in net profits), distributed over 5–8 years as exits occur.

**Balance sheet income** from the GP's own fund commitment (1–2% of fund size) generates returns identical to LP returns, providing additional economic alignment.

### Building an Institutional GP

The most successful PE firms have evolved from deal-doing partnerships into institutional businesses that can survive partner departures, attract institutional LP capital across fund generations, and scale their operations. Characteristics of institutional GPs include:

- Multi-generational team development (not dependent on a single founder)
- Formalised investment processes (IC protocols, DD standards, value creation frameworks)
- Dedicated fundraising and investor relations teams
- Portfolio operations capability (operating partners, functional experts)
- Brand and reputation that transcends individual partners
- Compliance and regulatory infrastructure
