### Reporting Standards

The PE industry has converged on common reporting standards, driven by LP demand for consistency and comparability:

**ILPA Reporting Template.** The Institutional Limited Partners Association (ILPA) has published standardised reporting templates that cover quarterly fund performance, capital account statements, fee and expense reporting, and portfolio company summaries. Most institutional LPs now expect GP reports to follow ILPA standards or closely equivalent formats.

**IPEV Valuation Guidelines.** The International Private Equity and Venture Capital Valuation (IPEV) Guidelines provide the standard framework for PE fund valuations. The guidelines require fair value measurement consistent with IFRS 13 and ASC 820 (US GAAP fair value standards). Valuation methodologies include:

- **Comparable company multiples** — applying public market multiples to the portfolio company's financial metrics
- **Precedent transaction multiples** — using recent M&A transaction multiples
- **Discounted cash flow** — for businesses with predictable, long-term cash flows
- **Calibration to entry price** — in early-stage investments where insufficient time has passed to justify a different valuation

### Valuation Controversy

PE fund valuations are inherently subjective. GPs have an economic incentive to value portfolio companies aggressively — higher NAV improves reported performance, which supports the next fundraise. LPs must evaluate whether GP valuations are conservative, fair, or aggressive relative to comparable public company multiples and recent transaction evidence.

Common valuation red flags:
- Portfolio companies consistently valued at or above entry price with no operational improvement
- Valuations that never decline even when market comparables have fallen
- Large upward revaluations immediately before a fundraise
- Methodologies that change between reporting periods without explanation

### LP Transparency Expectations

Modern LPs expect:
- Quarterly reports within 60–90 days of quarter-end
- Annual audited financial statements within 120 days of year-end
- Real-time access to an LP portal with capital account data, distribution notices, and fund documents
- Annual LP meetings with management presentations and portfolio company updates
- ESG reporting aligned with PRI, TCFD, or SFDR frameworks
