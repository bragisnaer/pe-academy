**Co-investment** is the opportunity for LPs to invest directly alongside the GP in a specific deal, typically on reduced or zero-fee terms. Co-investment has grown significantly in the last decade as LPs seek to increase PE exposure while reducing blended fee drag.

### How Co-Investment Works

When a GP identifies a deal that exceeds the fund's single-deal concentration limit, or is simply too large for the fund alone, the GP offers the excess allocation to LPs as a co-investment opportunity.

**For LPs:** Co-investment provides lower fees (often zero management fee and zero or reduced carry), direct deal-level transparency, and increased PE exposure without committing to an additional fund. Large LPs like CPP Investments, GIC, and Temasek have built dedicated co-investment teams.

**For GPs:** Co-investment allows the firm to pursue larger deals than the fund alone could support, strengthens LP relationships (by sharing attractive opportunities), and demonstrates alignment (the GP is sharing its best deals, not keeping them exclusive).

### Co-Investment Risks

- **Adverse selection** — are GPs offering co-investments on their best deals or on deals they are less confident about? Empirical evidence is mixed; some studies suggest co-investments underperform fund investments, while others show comparable returns.
- **Speed of execution** — co-investment decisions must be made quickly (often within 1–2 weeks). LPs without dedicated co-investment teams may not have the resources to conduct adequate due diligence.
- **Concentration risk** — co-investments increase LP exposure to individual deals, reducing diversification.
