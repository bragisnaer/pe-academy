PE firms operate within an increasingly complex regulatory environment. Understanding the regulatory landscape is essential for anyone working in fund management, compliance, or investor relations.

### US Regulation

**Dodd-Frank Act (2010)** requires PE firms with $150 million or more in AUM to register as investment advisers with the SEC. Registered advisers are subject to periodic examination, must maintain written compliance policies, and must file Form PF (private fund reporting) providing detailed fund-level data to the SEC.

**SEC Marketing Rule (2022)** governs how PE firms market their track records, requiring substantiation of performance claims and specific disclosures when presenting hypothetical or extracted performance.

### European Regulation

**AIFMD (Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive)** is the primary EU regulatory framework for PE fund managers. AIFMD requires: registration with a national regulator, minimum capital requirements, appointment of an independent depositary, detailed reporting to regulators (Annex IV reporting), and compliance with marketing rules when offering funds to EU investors. AIFMD II (effective 2026) introduces additional requirements around delegation, liquidity management, and loan-originating funds.

**SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation)** requires PE fund managers to classify their funds under Article 6 (no sustainability integration), Article 8 (promoting environmental or social characteristics), or Article 9 (sustainable investment objective). This classification affects marketing, reporting, and LP demand.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA)** — the governing document of a PE fund, defining fees, carry, governance, and investment restrictions
- **Placement agent** — an intermediary that helps GPs access LP networks during fundraising
- **Key-person clause** — a provision suspending the investment period if named senior partners depart the firm
- **Co-investment** — the opportunity for LPs to invest alongside the GP in a specific deal, typically on reduced fee terms
- **AIFMD** — the EU Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive; the primary regulatory framework for PE fund managers in Europe
- **SFDR** — the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation; governs sustainability disclosures for fund managers
