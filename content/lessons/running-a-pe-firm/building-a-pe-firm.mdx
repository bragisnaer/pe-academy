Starting and scaling a PE firm is one of the most difficult entrepreneurial challenges in finance. The vast majority of first-time fund managers fail to raise a second fund.

### The First Fund Challenge

First-time fund managers face a circular problem: LPs want to see a track record before committing, but the manager cannot build a track record without LP capital. Common solutions:

- **GP commit** — the founding partners invest significant personal capital (often $10–25M or more) to demonstrate conviction
- **Anchor LP** — securing one large, credible institutional investor as a "anchor" commit. Once a respected pension fund or endowment commits, other LPs follow more readily
- **Seed deal** — completing one or two deals (often with personal capital or a family office) before raising the fund, demonstrating deal sourcing capability and access
- **Placement agent** — engaging a placement agent with relationships across the LP universe

First-time funds are typically smaller ($100M–$500M), charge standard or slightly reduced fees, and often include more LP-friendly governance terms to attract early commitments.

### Scaling the Platform

The transition from Fund I to Fund III is where most PE firms either institutionalise or stall. Characteristics of successful scaling:

- **Repeatable process** — investment processes, DD standards, and value creation playbooks that work consistently across deal teams and vintages
- **Team depth** — developing a bench of next-generation partners who can source, lead, and manage deals independently
- **Fundraising capability** — building a dedicated investor relations team rather than relying on the founding partners to raise capital
- **Operational infrastructure** — compliance, finance, IT, and operations teams that support fund management at scale
- **Brand and reputation** — developing a market position that attracts deal flow, LP capital, and talent

### Key Terms Introduced in This Supplement

- **ILPA Reporting Template** — standardised LP reporting format published by the Institutional Limited Partners Association
- **IPEV Guidelines** — the standard framework for PE fund valuations
- **Side letter** — individual terms negotiated between a specific LP and the GP, supplementing the LPA
- **MFN (Most Favoured Nation) clause** — a side letter provision ensuring the LP receives terms at least as favourable as any other LP
- **Anchor LP** — the first large institutional investor to commit to a fund, signalling credibility to other LPs
- **Fairness opinion** — an independent assessment that a transaction price is fair to the affected parties
