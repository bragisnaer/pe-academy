MOIC — Multiple on Invested Capital — is the simplest measure of private equity return: how many times did we get back for every dollar we put in? A 3x MOIC means $1 invested returned $3.

Unlike IRR, MOIC ignores the time dimension entirely — a 3x return over 3 years and a 3x return over 10 years show the same MOIC but very different annualised performance — which is why the two metrics are always used together in PE reporting.

This lesson explains how MOIC is calculated (total value realised + remaining fair value, divided by invested capital), where it appears in fund reporting and LP communications, and how GPs use gross vs net MOIC distinctions when marketing fund performance.
