Revenue recognition determines when and how revenue appears in the financial statements. For PE investors, the critical question is whether reported revenue reflects genuine economic activity or is inflated by aggressive accounting choices.

### The Five-Step Framework (IFRS 15 / ASC 606)

Both IFRS and US GAAP use the same converged framework:

1. **Identify the contract** with the customer
2. **Identify the performance obligations** (distinct goods or services promised)
3. **Determine the transaction price** (including variable consideration, discounts, penalties)
4. **Allocate the transaction price** to each performance obligation
5. **Recognise revenue** when (or as) each performance obligation is satisfied

Revenue is recognised either **at a point in time** (when control transfers — typical for product sales) or **over time** (as the service is delivered — typical for long-term contracts, subscriptions, and construction).

### Where Revenue Recognition Gets Manipulated

**Aggressive percentage-of-completion.** Long-term contracts (construction, engineering, defence) recognise revenue based on the percentage of the contract completed. By overestimating the percentage complete or underestimating remaining costs, a company can accelerate revenue recognition.

**Channel stuffing.** Pushing excess inventory to distributors near quarter-end to inflate reported revenue. The distributor may return the goods in the next quarter, but the current quarter's revenue looks strong.

**Bill-and-hold arrangements.** Recognising revenue on goods that have been invoiced but not delivered. Under IFRS 15, bill-and-hold revenue can only be recognised if specific criteria are met (customer has requested the arrangement, risk has passed, the goods are separately identifiable).

**Premature contract recognition.** Recognising revenue on contracts that are not yet signed or where conditions for recognition are not met.

### What PE Buyers Should Check

- Compare revenue growth to cash collection (if revenue grows but receivables grow faster, revenue may be being recognised prematurely)
- Review the ageing of receivables (old receivables may indicate disputed or unrecoverable revenue)
- Assess revenue seasonality (is the quarterly pattern consistent, or does Q4 revenue spike suspiciously?)
- Review management's judgements on percentage-of-completion contracts
- Check for large year-end journal entries that accelerate revenue
