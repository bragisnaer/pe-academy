When a PE firm acquires a company, the purchase price is allocated across the target's identifiable assets and liabilities at fair value. The excess of the purchase price over the fair value of identified net assets is recorded as **goodwill**.

### The PPA Process

Under IFRS 3 (Business Combinations) and ASC 805 (US GAAP equivalent), the acquirer must:

1. Identify all tangible assets (PP&E, inventory, receivables) and measure at fair value
2. Identify all intangible assets separately from goodwill (customer relationships, brand/trademark, technology, non-compete agreements) and measure at fair value
3. Measure all liabilities at fair value (including contingent liabilities)
4. Calculate goodwill as: Purchase price – Fair value of identified net assets

### Impact on PE Financial Statements

**Intangible asset amortisation.** Identified intangible assets (customer relationships, technology) are amortised over their useful lives (typically 5–15 years). This amortisation is a non-cash charge that reduces reported net income but does not affect EBITDA or cash flow. PE firms add back acquisition-related amortisation when calculating adjusted EBITDA.

**Goodwill treatment:**

> **Market-specific note:** IFRS and US GAAP differ on goodwill.

- **IFRS:** Goodwill is not amortised. It is tested annually for impairment (IAS 36). If the carrying value of goodwill exceeds its recoverable amount, an impairment charge is recognised. Impairment is a non-cash charge but signals that the acquisition may have overpaid.
- **US GAAP:** For public companies, goodwill is tested for impairment (ASC 350). For private companies, an election is available to amortise goodwill over 10 years (ASU 2014-02), which simplifies accounting but creates a non-cash charge.

### Why This Matters for PE

**At acquisition:** PPA determines the amortisation charges that will reduce reported net income (but not EBITDA) during the hold period. A PPA that allocates a large portion of the purchase price to short-lived intangibles (e.g., 5-year customer relationships) creates high annual amortisation charges.

**At exit:** The next buyer will conduct their own PPA. If the exit buyer is a strategic acquirer, they may be willing to pay a premium for intangibles (brand, customer relationships) that the PE fund's PPA had amortised away. This means that book value (after amortisation) underestimates the business's fair value.
