> **Market-specific: EU member states, including Denmark**

### The EU Restructuring Directive (2019/1023)

The EU Restructuring Directive, implemented by member states between 2021 and 2022, introduced a harmonised framework for preventive restructuring across the EU. Key features:

**Preventive restructuring frameworks.** Member states must provide a framework allowing viable but financially distressed companies to restructure debts before formal insolvency. The framework includes:
- A moratorium on creditor enforcement (typically 4 months, extendable to 12 months)
- The ability to propose a restructuring plan to creditors
- Cross-class cramdown (imposing the plan on dissenting creditor classes) if the plan meets fairness tests

**Stay of enforcement.** Like Chapter 11's automatic stay, the EU framework provides a moratorium during which creditors cannot enforce their claims while the restructuring plan is being negotiated.

### Denmark: Rekonstruktion

> **Market-specific: Denmark**

Denmark implemented the EU Restructuring Directive through amendments to the Danish Bankruptcy Act (Konkursloven). The Danish restructuring framework (rekonstruktion) provides:

- **Court-supervised restructuring** — a company files for rekonstruktion at the local court (skifteretten). A restructuring administrator (rekonstruktør) is appointed, typically a licensed insolvency practitioner.
- **Moratorium** — creditors are stayed from enforcing claims during the restructuring period
- **Two tracks:** The restructuring can proceed through either a voluntary arrangement (tvangsakkord) with creditors or a business transfer (virksomhedsoverdragelse) — a sale of the business as a going concern
- **Creditor voting** — a restructuring plan requires approval by a majority of creditors (by value)
- **Employee protection** — the Danish Act on Employees' Rights in the Event of Business Transfer (virksomhedsoverdragelsesloven) implements the EU Acquired Rights Directive, protecting employee terms in a business transfer

### Germany: InsO and StaRUG

> **Market-specific: Germany**

Germany's Insolvency Code (InsO) provides a self-administration option (Eigenverwaltung) where management retains control under court supervision — the closest German equivalent to Chapter 11's debtor-in-possession model. The 2021 StaRUG (Stabilisierungs- und Restrukturierungsrahmen) implemented the EU Restructuring Directive, providing a pre-insolvency restructuring framework with cross-class cramdown for the first time.

### France: Procédure de Sauvegarde

> **Market-specific: France**

France's sauvegarde (safeguard) procedure allows a company to restructure debts while remaining under management control. The court appoints a mandataire judiciaire (judicial trustee) to represent creditor interests. Sauvegarde is debtor-friendly and can be initiated before the company is technically insolvent.
