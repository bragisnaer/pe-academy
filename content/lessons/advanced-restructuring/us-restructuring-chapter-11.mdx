> **Market-specific: United States**

**Chapter 11** of the US Bankruptcy Code is the most debtor-friendly restructuring framework in the world. The company (debtor) retains control of its operations while restructuring under court supervision.

### Key Features

**Automatic stay.** Upon filing Chapter 11, all creditor enforcement actions (lawsuits, foreclosures, collection efforts) are automatically frozen. This gives the debtor breathing room to develop a restructuring plan.

**Debtor-in-possession (DIP).** The existing management team remains in control of the company (unlike many European systems where an administrator takes control). This incentivises management to file early rather than waiting until the business is irreparably damaged.

**DIP financing.** New financing provided to the company during Chapter 11, with super-priority status (repaid before all pre-petition creditors). DIP financing keeps the business operating while the restructuring plan is developed. DIP loans are among the safest debt instruments because of their super-priority and typically earn attractive returns.

**Cramdown.** If certain classes of creditors reject the restructuring plan, the court can impose the plan over their objections (cramdown) provided the plan is "fair and equitable" (respects the absolute priority rule) and at least one impaired class has accepted it.

**Plan of reorganisation.** The restructuring plan specifies how each class of creditors will be treated: full payment, partial payment, debt-for-equity conversion, or zero recovery. Once confirmed by the court, the plan is binding on all parties.

### Timeline

A typical Chapter 11 restructuring takes 6–18 months from filing to emergence. Pre-packaged bankruptcies (where the plan is negotiated with major creditors before filing) can emerge in 30–60 days.
