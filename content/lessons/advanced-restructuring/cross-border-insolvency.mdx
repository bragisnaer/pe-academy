PE portfolio companies often operate across multiple jurisdictions. When restructuring is needed, the question of which country's insolvency framework applies is critical.

### EU Insolvency Regulation (Recast)

Within the EU, the European Insolvency Regulation determines which member state has jurisdiction over insolvency proceedings based on the company's **centre of main interests (COMI)** — typically the registered office unless the company's actual management is conducted elsewhere.

**Main proceedings** are opened in the COMI state and apply to the company's worldwide assets. **Secondary proceedings** can be opened in other member states where the company has an establishment, but only affecting assets in that state.

### UNCITRAL Model Law

Outside the EU, cross-border insolvency is governed by the UNCITRAL Model Law on Cross-Border Insolvency (adopted by the US, UK, and many other jurisdictions). The Model Law provides a framework for recognising foreign insolvency proceedings and coordinating parallel proceedings across jurisdictions.

### Practical Implications for PE

- A PE portfolio company incorporated in Luxembourg (a common holding jurisdiction) but managed from London with operations in Germany may face competing claims about where restructuring proceedings should be opened
- The choice of jurisdiction can significantly affect the outcome for different creditor classes (Chapter 11 is more debtor/equity-friendly; UK administration is more creditor-friendly)
- PE firms should consider insolvency jurisdiction as part of initial deal structuring — the holding company's domicile affects which restructuring framework applies if things go wrong

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Absolute priority rule** — senior claims must be satisfied before junior claims receive anything in a restructuring
- **Fulcrum security** — the tranche of debt that is partially but not fully covered by enterprise value; typically converts to equity
- **DIP financing** — new financing provided during Chapter 11 with super-priority status
- **Cramdown** — court imposition of a restructuring plan on dissenting creditor classes
- **Moratorium** — a stay preventing creditors from enforcing claims during restructuring
- **Pre-pack** — a pre-arranged sale of a company's business completed immediately upon entering administration
- **COMI** — Centre of Main Interests; determines which EU member state has jurisdiction over insolvency proceedings
- **Rekonstruktion** — the Danish court-supervised restructuring procedure under the Bankruptcy Act
