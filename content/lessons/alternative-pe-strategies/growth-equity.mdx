**Growth equity** occupies the space between venture capital and traditional buyouts. Growth equity investors take minority or significant minority stakes (typically 15–40%) in established, profitable businesses that need capital to accelerate growth but do not want or need a full buyout.

### Characteristics of Growth Equity

- **Minority positions** — the existing founders or management team retain control
- **No or minimal leverage** — growth equity deals are typically funded entirely with equity, not debt
- **High growth** — target companies are growing revenue at 20%+ annually
- **Profitability** — unlike venture-backed companies, growth equity targets typically have positive EBITDA or are near profitability
- **Capital for expansion** — proceeds fund geographic expansion, product development, sales team scaling, or technology investment

### Growth Equity Return Profile

Because growth equity uses little or no leverage, returns must come from revenue and earnings growth combined with multiple expansion. Growth equity funds typically target 25–35% gross IRR with 3–5x MOIC, achieved primarily through rapid revenue and earnings growth rather than financial engineering.

### Key Growth Equity Firms

General Atlantic, TA Associates, Summit Partners, Insight Partners, Warburg Pincus (growth-focused strategies), and Providence Equity are among the largest dedicated growth equity investors globally. In Europe, growth equity has expanded rapidly, with firms like Hg Capital (software-focused), Nordic Capital, and General Atlantic's European operations particularly active.
