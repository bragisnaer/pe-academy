Real estate PE invests in commercial real estate assets (office, retail, industrial, residential, hospitality) using leveraged fund structures similar to buyout PE.

### Key Differences from Corporate PE

- **Asset-based returns.** Real estate returns are driven by rental income (yield) and property value appreciation, not operating business earnings. Cap rates (net operating income / property value) are the equivalent of EBITDA multiples.
- **Higher leverage.** Real estate PE typically uses 60–75% leverage (higher than corporate PE) because real estate cash flows are more predictable and assets provide tangible collateral.
- **Longer cycles.** Real estate cycles are longer (7–15 years peak to peak) and more pronounced than corporate earnings cycles.
- **Location-specific risk.** Real estate value is inherently local. A building in central London has different risk characteristics from a similar building in a regional UK city.

### Real Estate PE Strategies

**Core** (6–8% target returns): stabilised, fully leased assets in prime locations. Low risk, income-oriented.
**Core-plus** (8–12%): slightly higher risk — assets requiring minor improvements or with some vacancy.
**Value-add** (12–18%): assets requiring significant repositioning, renovation, or leasing effort. The most "PE-like" strategy.
**Opportunistic** (18%+): development, distressed assets, or major repositioning. Highest risk and return.

### Key Real Estate PE Firms

Blackstone Real Estate, Brookfield Asset Management, Starwood Capital, KKR Real Estate, Hines, and AEW Capital are among the largest global players.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Supplement

- **Corporate carve-out** — acquisition of a division or business unit from a larger corporation
- **Transitional Service Agreement (TSA)** — an agreement for the seller to provide operational support to the carved-out business post-closing
- **Stranded costs** — corporate costs that do not disappear when a division is sold, remaining with the parent
- **Take-private (P2P)** — acquisition of a publicly listed company by a PE firm, resulting in delisting
- **Certain funds** — a financing provision (common in UK take-privates) requiring the buyer to demonstrate fully committed financing
- **Cap rate** — net operating income divided by property value; the real estate equivalent of an EBITDA yield
