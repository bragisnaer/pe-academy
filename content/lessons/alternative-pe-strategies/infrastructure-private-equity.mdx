**Infrastructure PE** invests in essential physical assets: transportation (airports, toll roads, ports), utilities (water, electricity, gas), telecommunications (fibre networks, data centres, towers), and social infrastructure (hospitals, schools, government buildings).

### Infrastructure as an Asset Class

Infrastructure investments offer characteristics that appeal to long-horizon investors:

- **Predictable, inflation-linked cash flows** — many infrastructure assets have regulated or contracted revenue streams indexed to inflation
- **Essential services** — demand for infrastructure services is relatively inelastic; people need water, electricity, and transportation regardless of economic conditions
- **Long asset lives** — infrastructure assets can operate for 30–50+ years
- **High barriers to entry** — regulatory permits, capital intensity, and natural monopoly characteristics protect existing assets from competition

### Infrastructure PE vs. Core Infrastructure

**Core infrastructure** funds (targeting 6–10% net returns) invest in operational, de-risked assets with stable cash flows — acquiring an existing toll road or utility.

**Infrastructure PE** (targeting 12–18% net returns) takes a more active approach: acquiring undermanaged infrastructure assets, building new assets (greenfield development), or transforming operations to improve returns. This approach requires more operational capability and accepts more risk.

### Key Infrastructure PE Firms

Brookfield Asset Management, Macquarie Infrastructure, Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP, acquired by BlackRock in 2024), EQT Infrastructure, KKR Infrastructure, and Ardian Infrastructure are among the largest players.
