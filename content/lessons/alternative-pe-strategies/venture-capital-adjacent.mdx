Venture capital (VC) and private equity share a common LP base and many structural similarities, but the investment approach, risk profile, and return drivers are fundamentally different. Understanding VC — especially its intersection with PE — is important because growth equity and late-stage VC increasingly overlap, and many PE firms (KKR, TPG, Carlyle) have launched growth and venture strategies.

### How VC Differs from PE

| Dimension | Venture Capital | Private Equity (Buyout) |
|-----------|----------------|------------------------|
| Stage | Early to growth | Mature |
| Ownership | Minority (10–30%) | Majority (50–100%) |
| Leverage | None | Significant |
| Revenue profile | Pre-revenue to high-growth | Stable, profitable |
| Return driver | Revenue growth, IPO/M&A exit at high multiples | EBITDA growth, leverage, multiple expansion |
| Loss rate | 50–70% of deals lose money | 15–25% of deals lose money |
| Return distribution | Power law (a few home runs drive fund returns) | More normally distributed |
| Hold period | 5–10 years | 3–7 years |

### The Convergence Zone: Growth Equity

Growth equity sits between VC and PE, combining elements of both. Growth equity targets companies that are past the VC stage (revenue-generating, often profitable) but earlier in their lifecycle than traditional buyout targets. The convergence means that PE buyout firms, growth equity specialists, and late-stage VC firms increasingly compete for the same deals — particularly in enterprise software, healthcare technology, and financial technology.
