Environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations have moved from a peripheral concern to a central element of PE investment processes, driven by LP demand, regulation, and the recognition that ESG factors can materially affect enterprise value.

### ESG Integration in PE

Most PE firms now integrate ESG analysis into their investment process at three stages:

**Pre-investment.** ESG due diligence identifies material environmental risks (pollution, climate exposure), social risks (labour practices, supply chain ethics), and governance risks (board composition, related-party transactions, compliance culture). ESG red flags can reduce the acquisition price or kill the deal entirely.

**During ownership.** PE firms implement ESG improvement initiatives: reducing carbon emissions, improving workplace safety, strengthening governance frameworks, and meeting sustainability reporting requirements. These improvements can reduce operational costs, mitigate regulatory risk, and enhance the business's attractiveness to future buyers.

**At exit.** ESG credentials increasingly influence exit valuations. Strategic buyers and public market investors assign premium valuations to businesses with strong ESG profiles. An ESG-compliant company is easier to sell and may command a higher multiple.

### Regulatory Drivers

**EU SFDR** requires PE fund managers to classify funds by sustainability commitment and disclose how ESG risks are integrated.

**EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)** (effective 2024–2026) requires large companies, including many PE portfolio companies, to report on sustainability metrics using European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS).

**UN Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI)** — over 5,000 signatories globally, including most institutional LPs. PRI membership is increasingly a threshold requirement for LP commitments.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Growth equity** — minority investment in established, high-growth, profitable businesses
- **Private credit** — direct lending by non-bank funds to companies, typically for LBO financing
- **Distressed investing** — acquiring companies or their debt at significant discounts during financial stress
- **Loan-to-own** — acquiring distressed debt with the intention of converting it to equity through restructuring
- **Infrastructure PE** — private equity investment in essential physical assets (transport, utilities, telecoms)
- **Secondaries** — the buying and selling of existing PE fund interests or direct company stakes on the secondary market
- **ESG** — environmental, social, and governance factors integrated into investment analysis and portfolio management
