No single valuation method is sufficient. PE professionals use multiple approaches in combination, each serving a different purpose.

### When to Use Each Method

| Method | Best for | Limitations |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Trading comps | Establishing market-based valuation floor; liquid, data-rich sectors | Reflects minority/liquid value; requires good public comps |
| Precedent transactions | Defending entry pricing; understanding what buyers have paid | Data can be stale; synergy-driven prices may not apply to financial buyers |
| DCF | Cross-checking; asset-heavy businesses; situations with few public comps | Highly sensitive to assumptions; terminal value dominates |
| LBO model | Determining ability to pay; pricing PE-specific returns | Dependent on financing availability and operating assumptions |

### The "Football Field" Chart

PE firms typically present valuation analysis as a horizontal bar chart (called a "football field" because of its shape) showing the implied valuation range from each method:

- Trading comps: €450–550M enterprise value
- Precedent transactions: €500–600M
- DCF: €420–520M
- LBO (at 20% target IRR): maximum €480M

The overlap zone — where multiple methods agree — typically defines the negotiating range.

### Common Valuation Pitfalls

**1. Anchoring to the seller's number.** Sellers present a valuation based on run-rate adjusted EBITDA at a premium exit multiple. Buyers should build their own view from verified financials.

**2. Ignoring the cycle.** Multiples expand in bull markets and compress in downturns. Paying a peak multiple at a peak EBITDA level produces the worst possible entry point.

**3. Confusing enterprise value and equity value.** This error sounds elementary but occurs more frequently than it should, particularly in cross-border transactions with different conventions.

**4. Over-reliance on EBITDA multiples for all businesses.** Some businesses are better valued on revenue (high-growth software), gross profit (marketplaces), or asset value (real estate, infrastructure).

### Key Terms Introduced in This Lesson

- **Football field chart** — a visual representation of valuation ranges from multiple methodologies
- **Ability to pay** — the maximum price a PE buyer can pay and still achieve target returns
- **Cycle-adjusted valuation** — the practice of considering where in the economic cycle a valuation multiple sits
