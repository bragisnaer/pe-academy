PE firms do not simply assess the existing team — they actively build teams. The ability to recruit exceptional executives into portfolio companies is a structural competitive advantage.

### Building a Talent Network Before You Need It

The best PE firms maintain a network of executive talent (current and former CEOs, CFOs, COOs, and functional leaders) who they know, trust, and can deploy into portfolio companies when needed. This network is built over years through:

- Operating partner relationships (former executives who have worked with the PE firm)
- Executive search firm partnerships (retained relationships with 3–5 top search firms per sector)
- Industry conferences and events
- Portfolio company alumni (executives who performed well in previous portfolio companies)

When a portfolio company needs a new CFO, the PE firm can often identify 3–5 qualified candidates within days rather than starting a search from scratch.

### Common Recruitment Priorities

**CFO replacement.** The most frequent post-acquisition hire. Founder-led businesses often have a bookkeeper or controller rather than a strategic CFO. PE-backed companies need a CFO who can: manage a leveraged balance sheet, produce monthly management accounts within 10 business days, build rolling cash flow forecasts, manage lender relationships and covenant compliance, and support M&A (bolt-on diligence, integration, and financing).

**Commercial leadership.** Sales directors or Chief Commercial Officers who can build a professional sales function (pipeline management, CRM discipline, KPI-driven sales management) in companies that have historically relied on founder relationships or inbound demand.

**Operational leadership.** COOs or operations directors who can implement lean operations, manage buy-and-build integration, and drive cost efficiency.

### Onboarding and Retention

Recruiting an executive is only the beginning. The first 90 days of a new hire's tenure are critical:
- Clear mandate and performance expectations (documented, not assumed)
- Relationship-building with the PE board and operating partners
- Quick diagnostic of the team and operations (the new executive's independent assessment, separate from the DD view)
- Early-win identification (what can this person deliver in the first quarter to build credibility?)

Retention through the hold period is maintained through equity incentives (MEP), development support, and a governance relationship that balances challenge with support.
