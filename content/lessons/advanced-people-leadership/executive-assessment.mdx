PE returns depend more on the quality of portfolio company management than on any financial model. The assessment of whether a management team can execute a value creation plan under PE ownership is the most consequential judgement call in any deal process.

### What PE Firms Assess

**Track record.** Has this person grown a business before? Have they managed through a downturn? Have they worked in a PE-backed environment (with its reporting discipline, board scrutiny, and leverage constraints)?

**Cognitive ability.** Can they think clearly about complex, ambiguous problems? Can they separate signal from noise? Do they understand their market deeply enough to make sound strategic decisions?

**Drive and resilience.** PE ownership is intense. Monthly board reporting, quarterly KPI reviews, and constant pressure to deliver results test the most capable executives. The assessment must determine whether the executive thrives under this pressure or breaks.

**Self-awareness.** Does the executive understand their own strengths and weaknesses? Will they accept help (from operating partners, consultants, or new hires) in areas where they are weak? Or will ego prevent them from building the team they need?

**Alignment.** Is the executive willing to invest personal capital in the MEP (management equity programme)? Willingness to co-invest is one of the strongest signals of management confidence. Reluctance is a red flag.

### Assessment Methods

**Structured interviews.** Move beyond generic questions. Focus on specific situations: "Tell me about a time you had to cut costs while growing revenue simultaneously. What did you do? What did you sacrifice? What was the result?"

**Reference calls.** The most valuable references are those the candidate did not suggest. Former direct reports, former board members, industry peers, and customers provide a 360-degree view that the candidate's own narrative may not reflect.

**Psychometric and behavioural profiling.** Some PE firms use tools like Hogan Assessment, DISC profiles, or Korn Ferry's leadership assessment to supplement interview-based evaluation. These tools are not definitive, but they surface patterns (risk aversion, interpersonal style, resilience under pressure) that inform the overall assessment.

**Operating partner assessment.** Former CEOs or CFOs within the PE firm's operating partner network assess the management team through a practitioner lens. An operating partner who has run a similar business can evaluate operational capability in ways that a financial professional cannot.

### The "Would You Back This CEO?" Test

The simplest assessment framework: after completing diligence, ask every deal team member the question: "If we handed this CEO €50 million of your personal savings and asked them to grow it over 5 years, would you trust them with it?" If the honest answer is no, the deal should not proceed without a management change plan.
