### KPI-Driven Management

PE-backed companies operate with a discipline level that exceeds most privately held businesses. Monthly KPI reviews replace the annual budgeting cycle as the primary management tool.

**Financial KPIs (reported monthly):**
- Revenue (actual vs. budget, variance analysis)
- EBITDA and EBITDA margin (with explanation of any margin movement)
- Cash flow and covenant compliance
- Working capital metrics (DSO, DIO, DPO)
- Order intake and pipeline (leading indicators of future revenue)

**Operational KPIs (sector-specific):**
- Software: ARR, churn, NRR, CAC payback, LTV/CAC ratio
- Healthcare: patient volumes, staffing ratios, wait times, clinical outcomes
- Manufacturing: OEE, defect rates, output per employee, on-time delivery
- Services: utilisation, employee turnover, customer satisfaction (NPS)

### The Monthly Board Pack

The monthly board pack is the primary reporting mechanism. A well-structured pack includes:

1. **Executive summary** (1 page): headline KPIs, key developments, items requiring board attention
2. **Financial summary** (2–3 pages): P&L, balance sheet, cash flow — actual vs. budget vs. prior year
3. **Operational review** (2–3 pages): key operational KPIs with trend analysis
4. **Value creation tracker** (1–2 pages): status of each value creation initiative against plan
5. **Pipeline and commercial update** (1–2 pages): sales pipeline, key customer developments, competitive intelligence
6. **Risk register** (1 page): top 5 risks with mitigation actions and owners
7. **Cash flow forecast** (1 page): rolling 13-week cash flow forecast

The pack should be distributed 3–5 business days before the board meeting to allow board members to prepare questions.

### Managing Underperformance

PE firms must act quickly when a portfolio company or individual executive underperforms:

**The 30-60-90 framework:**
- **30 days:** Identify the problem. Is it market-driven, execution-driven, or people-driven? Gather data and conduct root-cause analysis.
- **60 days:** Implement a correction plan. If the issue is execution, provide support (operating partner involvement, additional resources, process changes). If the issue is people, begin the transition (recruit a replacement while supporting the incumbent).
- **90 days:** Assess results. If the correction plan is working, continue. If not, execute the change (replace the individual, pivot the strategy, restructure the cost base).

Waiting too long to address underperformance is one of the most common mistakes in PE portfolio management.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Executive assessment** — the structured evaluation of a portfolio company's management team against the requirements of the value creation plan
- **MEP (Management Equity Programme)** — the equity incentive structure aligning management and GP financial interests
- **Span of control** — the number of direct reports a manager supervises; fewer layers with wider spans improve communication and reduce cost
- **Functional professionalisation** — replacing informal, generalist roles with specialised functional leadership as a company scales
- **30-60-90 framework** — a structured timeline for diagnosing, correcting, and resolving underperformance
