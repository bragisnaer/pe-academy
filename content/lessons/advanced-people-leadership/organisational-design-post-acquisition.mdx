PE-backed companies often require organisational restructuring to execute the value creation plan. This goes beyond headcount reduction — it involves redesigning how the business operates.

### Common Organisational Changes

**Span of control optimisation.** Many mid-market companies have too many management layers relative to their size. A business with 200 employees and 6 management layers between the CEO and the front line has an inefficient structure. Reducing to 3–4 layers improves communication speed, accountability, and cost.

**Functional professionalisation.** Founder-led businesses often have informal, generalist roles ("Sarah handles marketing, HR, and the office"). PE-backed companies need specialised functional leadership: a dedicated HR leader, a marketing specialist, a procurement manager. This professionalisation enables scale.

**Shared services for buy-and-build.** Buy-and-build platforms need a shared services structure (finance, HR, IT, procurement) that serves all acquired businesses. Designing this structure before the first bolt-on is acquired avoids the chaos of building it on the fly.

**Governance structure.** Clear reporting lines, decision rights (what the CEO can decide alone vs. what requires board approval), and performance management systems. Many founder-led businesses have informal governance that is effective at small scale but breaks down as the company grows under PE ownership.

### Workforce Restructuring: Jurisdiction Notes

**Important:** Workforce restructuring (redundancies, role changes, site closures) is governed by employment law that varies significantly across jurisdictions.

- **Denmark:** The Danish Salaried Employees Act (Funktionærloven) governs notice periods. Collective redundancies of 10+ employees require notification to the Regional Employment Council (Det Regionale Arbejdsmarkedsråd) at least 30 days in advance. The Danish Act on Collective Redundancies (Lov om masseafskedigelser) implements the EU Collective Redundancies Directive.
- **Germany:** Works council (Betriebsrat) consultation is mandatory before any workforce restructuring. Social plans (Sozialplan) must be negotiated with the works council for larger-scale redundancies. Unfair dismissal protections are strong.
- **France:** CSE consultation is mandatory. The process for collective redundancies (Plan de Sauvegarde de l'Emploi, PSE) is complex and time-consuming. Approval from the labour inspectorate (DIRECCTE) is required.
- **UK:** Consultation periods of 30 days (20–99 redundancies) or 45 days (100+ redundancies) apply. TUPE protections apply for business transfers.
- **US:** At-will employment provides significantly more flexibility than European jurisdictions. The WARN Act requires 60 days' notice for plant closures or mass layoffs of 100+ employees.

PE firms operating across multiple European jurisdictions must factor employment law timelines and costs into their restructuring plans. What can be executed in 60 days in the US may take 6–12 months in Germany or France.
