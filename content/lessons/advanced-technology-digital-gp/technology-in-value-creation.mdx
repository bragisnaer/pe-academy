Beyond using technology internally, PE firms increasingly deploy technology as a value creation lever within portfolio companies.

### Common Technology Interventions

**CRM implementation.** Many mid-market companies manage customer relationships in spreadsheets or the founder's head. Implementing Salesforce, HubSpot, or a vertical-specific CRM provides pipeline visibility, sales process discipline, and the data infrastructure to optimise sales performance. This is often one of the highest-ROI technology investments.

**ERP consolidation.** Buy-and-build platforms frequently have multiple ERP systems inherited from each acquired company. Consolidating onto a single platform (SAP, Oracle NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics) enables standardised reporting, centralised procurement, and operational visibility across the group. This is expensive (€1–10M+ depending on scope) and time-consuming (12–24 months) but essential for integration.

**E-commerce and digital channels.** For B2B companies, launching or improving e-commerce capability can open new customer segments, reduce cost-to-serve, and capture transaction data for analytics. For B2C companies, direct-to-consumer (DTC) digital channels improve margins by bypassing wholesale intermediaries.

**Pricing technology.** Dynamic pricing, price optimisation, and CPQ (configure-price-quote) tools enable more sophisticated pricing than manual price lists. For businesses with thousands of SKUs and hundreds of customers, pricing technology can identify margin opportunities that are invisible to manual analysis.

**AI and automation.** Practical AI applications in portfolio companies (not theoretical):
- Demand forecasting (reducing inventory costs)
- Predictive maintenance (reducing downtime in manufacturing)
- Customer service automation (chatbots for tier-1 support)
- Document processing (invoice automation, compliance monitoring)
- Quality control (computer vision for defect detection in manufacturing)

### The PE Technology Operating Model

Some PE firms have centralised technology capabilities that serve their entire portfolio:

**Shared technology leadership.** A PE firm's technology operating partner or CTO works across all portfolio companies, providing expertise that no single mid-market company could afford independently.

**Group procurement.** The PE firm negotiates enterprise-wide agreements with technology vendors (Microsoft, Salesforce, AWS) at volume discounts that individual portfolio companies could not access.

**Playbook replication.** Technology interventions that worked in one portfolio company (CRM implementation, e-commerce build, pricing tool deployment) are documented and replicated across subsequent acquisitions.

### Case Illustration: Hg Capital's Technology Approach

Hg Capital, a London-based PE firm focused on software and services, has built a dedicated "Hg Capital Technology" team that works across its portfolio. The team provides portfolio companies with shared expertise in product development, cloud migration, cybersecurity, data analytics, and AI deployment. This centralised capability allows Hg to accelerate technology transformation across its portfolio in ways that smaller PE firms or individual portfolio companies cannot replicate.
