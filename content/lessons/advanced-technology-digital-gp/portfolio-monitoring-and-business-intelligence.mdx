Once a portfolio company is acquired, the PE firm needs real-time visibility into its performance. Traditional quarterly reporting is too slow for active ownership. Modern PE firms deploy portfolio monitoring platforms that aggregate data across all portfolio companies into a single dashboard.

### What Portfolio Monitoring Platforms Track

**Financial KPIs (monthly):**
- Revenue (actual vs. budget vs. prior year)
- EBITDA and EBITDA margin
- Free cash flow
- Leverage ratio and covenant headroom
- Working capital (DSO, DIO, DPO)
- Cash position and runway

**Operational KPIs (varies by sector):**
- Software: ARR, net revenue retention, customer churn, CAC payback period
- Healthcare: patient volumes, staffing ratios, reimbursement rates
- Industrial: order intake, backlog, capacity utilisation, OEE (overall equipment effectiveness)
- Services: utilisation rates, employee turnover, customer satisfaction (NPS)

**Value creation tracking:**
- Progress against the 100-day plan
- Status of each value creation initiative (on track, delayed, at risk)
- Bolt-on acquisition pipeline and integration status
- Management team assessment and succession planning

### Platform Options

PE-specific portfolio monitoring platforms include:
- **Chronograph** — LP and GP analytics platform
- **iLevel** (by MSCI) — portfolio company data aggregation
- **eFront** (by BlackRock) — alternative investment management software
- **Cobalt** (by Hg Capital's Hg technology platform) — operational analytics
- **Custom-built dashboards** — many large PE firms build proprietary monitoring platforms using tools like Tableau, Power BI, or Looker connected to portfolio company data feeds

### Data Quality Challenges

The biggest obstacle to effective portfolio monitoring is not the platform — it is the data. Many mid-market portfolio companies:
- Produce management accounts manually in Excel with a 3–4 week lag
- Do not track operational KPIs systematically
- Have different chart-of-accounts structures that make cross-portfolio comparison difficult
- Lack the IT infrastructure to provide automated data feeds

Building data quality at the portfolio company level is often a first-100-days priority. The PE firm may invest in ERP upgrades, reporting automation, or dedicated FP&A (financial planning and analysis) hires to ensure reliable, timely data.
