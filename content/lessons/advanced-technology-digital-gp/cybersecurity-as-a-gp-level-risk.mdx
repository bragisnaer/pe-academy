Cybersecurity is no longer only a portfolio company risk — it is a GP-level risk. PE firms themselves hold extremely sensitive data: LP personal information, fund financial data, deal pipeline information, portfolio company performance data, and merger discussions. A breach at the GP level can compromise LP trust, deal confidentiality, and regulatory standing.

### GP-Level Cybersecurity Requirements

- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all systems
- Encrypted communications for deal-sensitive information
- Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessment
- Employee cybersecurity training (phishing simulation, data handling procedures)
- Incident response plan (documented, tested, with clear escalation protocols)
- Third-party risk assessment (ensuring data room providers, advisers, and service providers meet security standards)

### Regulatory Expectations

The SEC has increased cybersecurity enforcement against registered investment advisers (including PE firms). AIFMD and national regulators in Europe expect appropriate data security and operational resilience. GDPR imposes specific obligations around data breach notification (72-hour reporting to the relevant data protection authority).

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Data-driven sourcing** — using AI and data analytics to identify potential acquisition targets from large datasets
- **Portfolio monitoring platform** — software that aggregates financial and operational data across multiple portfolio companies for the GP
- **CPQ (Configure-Price-Quote)** — technology enabling automated, optimised pricing for complex product and service offerings
- **Technical debt** — deferred IT maintenance and modernisation that creates future cost and risk
- **Shared technology leadership** — a PE firm's centralised technology capability serving multiple portfolio companies
