PE investment decisions are high-stakes, low-frequency events made under uncertainty. The information is always incomplete. The time pressure is real. The sums are large. These are exactly the conditions where cognitive biases have the most impact.

Academic research (Kahneman and Tversky's prospect theory, Thaler's behavioural economics, and Howard Marks's applied investment philosophy) demonstrates that even sophisticated professionals make systematic errors in judgement. The PE industry is not immune. Several studies have shown that PE deal outcomes correlate with behavioural patterns: overpaying in competitive auctions, holding losers too long, and deploying capital under pressure all reduce returns.

The purpose of this module is not to eliminate biases — that is impossible. It is to make them visible so that investment processes can be designed to counteract them.
