### Financial Red Flags

- Adjusted EBITDA 20%+ above reported (aggressive add-backs)
- Revenue recognition accelerating revenue before cash collection
- Declining cash conversion
- Unexplained accounting policy changes near a sale process
- Related-party transactions at non-market rates
- Consistent "one-time" costs every year
- Working capital deterioration
- Below-industry capex (deferred maintenance the buyer will fund)
- Auditor qualifications or changes

### Commercial Red Flags

- Single customer above 25% of revenue
- Declining win rates / shrinking pipeline
- Market share losses
- Single-product dependency approaching obsolescence
- Customer interviews contradicting management claims

### Legal Red Flags

- Material pending litigation
- Environmental contamination requiring remediation
- GDPR/FCPA non-compliance
- Unresolved change-of-control clauses on major contracts
- Unclear IP ownership

### Management Red Flags

- Key-person dependency
- Team unwilling to co-invest equity
- High senior turnover
- No succession plan
- Inconsistent information across diligence workstreams

### The Walk-Away Framework

**Thesis-critical?** Does the finding directly threaten the core investment thesis?
**Quantifiable or unquantifiable?** Can the risk be priced (price reduction, indemnity)? Or is downside unlimited?
**Fixable or structural?** Can it be remediated post-acquisition? Or is it a structural market/business problem?

If thesis-critical, unquantifiable, and structural — walk away.

### Exercise: Due Diligence Decision Matrix

**Task:** You have completed DD on a software company. Findings:

1. True EBITDA is €25M (vs. seller's €28M)
2. Top customer is 22% of revenue; contract expires in 18 months, no renewal certainty
3. CTO has resigned and is joining a competitor
4. GDPR compliance is incomplete (3 of 12 major customers missing DPAs)
5. Market growing 8% with regulatory tailwinds
6. FCF conversion 75% — strong
7. Bolt-on pipeline attractive: 4 targets at 5–7x EBITDA

Classify each as: (a) thesis-critical or not, (b) quantifiable or not, (c) fixable or structural. Then recommend: proceed (at what price?), renegotiate, or walk away.

### Key Terms Introduced

- **Quality of earnings (QofE)** — forensic analysis validating adjusted EBITDA
- **Debt-like items** — obligations deducted in the EV-to-equity bridge
- **Change-of-control clause** — contract provision triggered by ownership change
- **Deal killer** — finding severe enough to abandon the transaction
- **EBITDA bridge** — line-by-line reconciliation from reported to adjusted EBITDA
- **Technical debt** — deferred IT maintenance creating future cost obligations
