### Key Areas

**Corporate structure** — ownership chain, subsidiaries, minority interests. Affects tax efficiency (share vs. asset deal) and ability to push down acquisition debt.

**Material contracts** — change-of-control clauses (can terminate the deal if consent is not obtained), exclusivity provisions, minimum volume commitments, termination rights, IP assignment clauses.

**Intellectual property** — proper registration and ownership, infringement risks, open-source software licensing dependencies.

**Employment and labour** — executive agreements, collective bargaining, works council requirements (mandatory in Germany, France, Netherlands, and most EU countries before workforce changes), TUPE/Acquired Rights Directive protections, pension obligations.

**Regulatory compliance** — GDPR/data privacy, FCPA/UK Bribery Act, industry-specific licences, environmental permits, sanctions.

**Litigation** — pending/threatened lawsuits, regulatory investigations, historical settlement patterns.

### European Labour Complexity

In Europe, works council consultation is mandatory before material operational changes in many countries. German Betriebsrat, French CSE, and Dutch OR each have specific consultation rights and timelines. Failure to consult properly can delay restructuring or invalidate management decisions. TUPE and the Acquired Rights Directive protect employee terms upon business transfer — employees transfer automatically on existing terms.

### Tax Due Diligence

Historical tax compliance, transfer pricing documentation and defensibility, acquisition structuring (interest deductibility, withholding taxes), deferred tax assets/liabilities, and VAT/GST compliance. The OECD's BEPS framework and EU ATAD I/II limit aggressive structures.

### Environmental Due Diligence

Phase I/II environmental site assessments for businesses with manufacturing, real estate, or hazardous materials. Remediation costs can run into millions. Climate risk assessment (physical and transition risks) is increasingly standard.
