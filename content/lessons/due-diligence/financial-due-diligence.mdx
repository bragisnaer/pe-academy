Financial due diligence (FDD) validates whether the seller's numbers reflect reality. The centrepiece is the **quality of earnings (QofE)** analysis.

### Quality of Earnings Process

**Step 1: Verify reported financials.** Reconcile management accounts to audited financial statements.

**Step 2: Assess each EBITDA add-back.** For every add-back:

| Add-back Type | Typically Valid? | Key Test |
|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| Lawsuit settlement | Yes, if isolated | Pattern of litigation? |
| Restructuring costs | Partially | Finite programme, or recurring? |
| Owner above-market compensation | Yes, if owner is leaving | Replacement hired at market rate? |
| Non-recurring consulting fees | Often invalid | Present in prior years? |
| Share-based compensation | Debatable | New SBC granted post-close? |
| Run-rate savings | Partially | Contract signed? Savings verified? |

**Step 3: Identify items the seller did not adjust for.** Above-market related-party transactions, unsustainable revenue, temporarily depressed costs (deferred maintenance, unfilled vacancies).

**Step 4: Normalise for seasonality and cyclicality.**

**Step 5: Assess working capital.** Determine the normalised level. This feeds into the SPA working capital adjustment.

### Net Debt and Debt-Like Items

Every missed debt-like item transfers directly from the seller to the buyer's loss:
- Financial debt (including accrued interest on shareholder loans)
- Underfunded pension liabilities
- Capital lease obligations
- Deferred consideration from prior acquisitions (earn-outs)
- Outstanding tax liabilities
- Provisions for litigation or regulatory fines
- Transaction bonuses triggered by the sale
- Overdue trade payables (above normal levels)
- Environmental remediation obligations
- Customer prepayments representing future obligations

### Cash Flow Analysis

Beyond EBITDA, FDD analyses historical cash flow conversion. A business with strong EBITDA but weak cash conversion is a weaker LBO candidate. Key questions: consistent FCF generation? Capex cycles distorting the LTM picture? Working capital trending up (consuming cash) or down (releasing cash)?

### Who Conducts FDD?

Big Four (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG), specialist firms (Alvarez & Marsal, FTI Consulting). Cost: $300,000–$1,500,000. Timeline: 4–8 weeks.
