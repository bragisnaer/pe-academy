### Management Assessment

- In-depth interviews with CEO, CFO, key functional leaders — assessing execution capability, not just strategy
- 360-degree references from contacts the management team did not suggest
- Track record of execution under PE governance and leverage constraints
- Gap analysis: CFO capable of leveraged balance sheets? COO for operational improvement? CTO for digital transformation? Sales leadership for expansion?

PE firms assess management against the specific requirements of the value creation plan, not just general competence.

### Technology and IT Assessment

- Core systems (ERP, CRM, HR) — age, capability, scalability
- Cybersecurity — penetration testing, incident history, data protection compliance (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Technical debt — quantified cost of deferred IT maintenance
- Data quality — can the company produce accurate, timely management information?
- Integration capability — can the systems absorb bolt-on acquisitions?

### Supply Chain and Operations

- Supplier concentration and single-source dependencies
- Production capacity utilisation and expansion capability
- Quality management and defect rates
- Operational benchmarking (revenue per employee, output per machine hour, waste rates)

### Value Creation Plan Validation

The operating team assesses: are cost savings achievable (at what one-time cost)? Can pricing improvements stick? Is the bolt-on pipeline realistic? Does the organisation have capacity to execute while running the business? What are the key execution risks?

The output is a risk-adjusted value creation plan, not the optimistic version.
