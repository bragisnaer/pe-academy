Commercial due diligence (CDD) answers the most fundamental question in any PE deal: **is this a good business in an attractive market?**

### What CDD Covers

**Market analysis** — total addressable market (TAM), served addressable market (SAM), market growth rate, and key demand drivers. PE firms distinguish between secular growth markets (structural demand trends driving long-term expansion) and cyclical markets (growth fluctuating with economic cycles). A healthcare technology company riding ageing demographics is a different proposition from an industrial distributor whose revenue correlates with manufacturing PMI.

**Competitive landscape** — market share, consolidation level, barriers to entry (regulatory licences, capital intensity, IP, brand equity, switching costs), supplier power, and substitution threats. A fragmented market presents both risk (intense competition) and opportunity (buy-and-build consolidation).

**Customer analysis** — concentration (percentage of revenue from top 5/10 customers), retention rates, contract structure, switching costs, and satisfaction. CDD advisers typically conduct 15–30 "voice of the customer" interviews — unfiltered feedback from the people who actually pay the company. These interviews are among the most valuable diligence inputs.

**Pricing power** — can the business raise prices without losing customers? CDD examines historical price increases (frequency, magnitude, customer response), competitive pricing benchmarking, and whether the business competes on price or value. Pricing power is the clearest signal of competitive advantage.

**Growth drivers** — organic (new products, geographies, cross-selling), pricing-driven, and inorganic (bolt-on acquisitions). Each pathway needs credibility and cost assessment.

### Who Conducts CDD?

Management consulting firms (McKinsey, Bain, BCG, L.E.K., OC&C, Kearney). Cost: $500,000–$2,000,000. Timeline: 4–8 weeks.

### CDD Output: The Market Map

A strong CDD report produces a market map showing total market size and growth, segments by end-market/geography/product, the target's position in each segment, the most attractive segments for future growth, and competitive threats.
