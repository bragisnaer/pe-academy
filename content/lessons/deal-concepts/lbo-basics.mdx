A leveraged buyout (LBO) is the acquisition of a company using a significant proportion of borrowed money — typically 50–70% of the purchase price — with the acquired company's assets and cash flows serving as collateral for the debt.

The LBO model is the defining transaction structure in private equity: debt amplifies returns on the equity invested (leverage effect), but also amplifies downside risk, making target selection, capital structure, and operational improvement critical to deal success.

This lesson introduces LBO mechanics — enterprise value, debt tranches (senior secured, unitranche, mezzanine), the equity bridge, and how changes in EBITDA growth, multiple expansion, and debt paydown each contribute to investor returns as measured by MOIC and IRR.
