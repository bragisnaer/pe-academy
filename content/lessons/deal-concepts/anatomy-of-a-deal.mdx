A private equity deal follows a structured process from initial thesis and sourcing through due diligence, financing, close, and ultimately exit — each stage involving distinct workstreams and participants.

The deal process typically begins with sourcing (proprietary deal flow or auction), moves through indicative offers, management presentations, and exclusivity, and then into confirmatory due diligence across commercial, financial, legal, and operational dimensions.

This lesson walks through every stage of the deal lifecycle, explains the key documents involved (NDA, teaser, CIM, LOI, SPA), and shows how the investment thesis is stress-tested at each stage — from initial EBITDA screening through to signing and closing conditions.
