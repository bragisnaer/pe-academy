### The Three-Stakeholder Test

Before any significant decision (dividend recap, restructuring, management change, exit), PE professionals should evaluate the decision against three stakeholder groups:

**1. LP investors.** Does this decision serve LP return objectives? This is the primary fiduciary duty.

**2. Portfolio company stakeholders.** Does this decision leave the business viable and its employees, customers, and suppliers fairly treated? While PE firms do not owe a fiduciary duty to portfolio company employees, the industry's social licence depends on responsible treatment.

**3. The GP firm's long-term reputation.** Will this decision, if it became public, damage the firm's ability to raise future funds, recruit talent, or win deals? The "newspaper test" — would you be comfortable if this decision appeared on the front page? — is a practical shortcut.

### When the Three Stakeholders Conflict

Conflicts are inevitable. A restructuring that maximises LP returns may require workforce reductions. A dividend recap that de-risks the fund's equity position may weaken the portfolio company's balance sheet. An exit to a buyer who will cut costs aggressively may not be in the management team's interest.

The ethical framework does not eliminate these tensions. It requires the PE professional to:
- Acknowledge the conflict explicitly (not pretend it does not exist)
- Assess the proportionality of the impact (a modest workforce reduction to improve efficiency is different from gutting a business to extract a dividend)
- Consider mitigation (severance packages, retraining support, phased implementation)
- Accept accountability for the consequences

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Commingling** — the illegal mixing of LP fund capital with GP corporate funds
- **Sale-leaseback** — selling a property and leasing it back, generating cash but creating ongoing lease obligations
- **Social licence** — the informal acceptance by society that a business or industry operates legitimately
- **Three-stakeholder test** — evaluating PE decisions against LP interests, portfolio company stakeholders, and the GP's long-term reputation
- **Walker Guidelines** — UK guidelines on transparency and disclosure for PE-backed companies
