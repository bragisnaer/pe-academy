The PE industry is evolving rapidly. Several structural shifts will reshape the industry over the next decade.

### Democratisation of Access

Historically, PE has been accessible only to institutional investors and ultra-high-net-worth individuals. That is changing:

- **Semi-liquid PE products** (offered by Blackstone, Apollo, KKR, and others) provide retail and high-net-worth investors with PE exposure through structures that offer periodic liquidity (quarterly or semi-annual redemptions with limits).
- **Listed PE vehicles** (like the London-listed PE investment trusts — HgCapital Trust, Pantheon International, ICG Enterprise Trust) provide public market access to PE portfolios.
- **Defined contribution pension plans** are beginning to allocate to PE, following regulatory changes that encourage illiquid asset inclusion in retirement savings products.

### Technology and AI in PE

PE firms are deploying technology across the investment lifecycle:

- **Deal sourcing** — AI and data analytics tools scan proprietary datasets to identify potential acquisition targets, track management changes, and monitor industry trends. Firms like EQT (through their "Motherbrain" AI platform) have invested significantly in proprietary sourcing technology.
- **Due diligence** — automated document review, data extraction, and financial analysis tools accelerate diligence timelines and reduce human error.
- **Portfolio monitoring** — real-time dashboards tracking operational KPIs, financial performance, and covenant compliance across all portfolio companies.
- **Value creation** — deploying AI within portfolio companies to improve operations (demand forecasting, pricing optimisation, customer service automation).

### Convergence of Alternative Strategies

The distinction between PE, private credit, infrastructure, and real estate is blurring. The largest alternative asset managers now offer the full spectrum, enabling institutional investors to allocate across strategies through a single GP relationship. This convergence creates massive platforms (Blackstone, Apollo, KKR) and makes it harder for smaller, specialised firms to compete for LP capital.

### Regulatory Tightening

Expect continued regulatory evolution:

- Enhanced transparency and reporting requirements from SEC and ESMA
- Stricter ESG disclosure mandates (CSRD, SFDR evolution)
- Increased scrutiny of PE ownership in sensitive sectors (healthcare, housing, financial services)
- Potential changes to carried interest tax treatment in multiple jurisdictions
