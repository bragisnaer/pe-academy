The first 100 days after closing an acquisition are the most critical period in the PE ownership cycle. The **100-day plan** is a structured action plan that translates the pre-acquisition investment thesis into a prioritised execution roadmap.

### Why 100 Days?

The 100-day window matters for three reasons:

**1. Momentum.** A new owner has a brief window of credibility and urgency. Management teams, employees, customers, and suppliers expect change. Delay signals indecision.

**2. Early wins.** Quick, visible improvements build confidence and demonstrate that PE ownership creates value. Early wins might include renegotiating a major supplier contract, filling a critical management gap, or launching a pricing initiative.

**3. Diagnostic accuracy.** The diligence process provides a view from outside. The first 100 days provide an inside view — PE firms often discover both additional opportunities and hidden problems that diligence could not fully surface.

### What the 100-Day Plan Covers

A typical 100-day plan includes:

**Governance and reporting** — establishing board composition, meeting cadence, reporting templates, and KPI dashboards. PE-backed boards typically meet monthly (not quarterly), with detailed financial and operational reporting packages.

**Management assessment** — confirming whether the existing team can execute the value creation plan, or whether changes are needed. It is not uncommon for PE firms to replace the CFO within the first 100 days, installing a finance leader experienced with leveraged balance sheets and PE reporting requirements.

**Quick-win initiatives** — cost reduction actions (procurement savings, headcount optimisation, facility rationalisation) that can deliver measurable savings within the first quarter. These are typically identified during diligence and validated in the first weeks of ownership.

**Strategic priorities** — the medium-term growth and operational improvement initiatives: pricing strategy changes, sales force expansion, geographic entry, bolt-on acquisition pipeline activation.

**Financial controls** — implementing cash flow forecasting, working capital monitoring, covenant compliance tracking, and monthly management accounts aligned with PE reporting standards.
