Cost reduction is the fastest and most predictable value creation lever. Unlike revenue growth (which depends on markets and customers), cost savings are within the PE firm's direct control.

### Common Cost Levers

**Procurement** — centralising purchasing, renegotiating supplier contracts, and leveraging increased scale (particularly after bolt-on acquisitions). A 3–5% reduction in direct material costs can translate to significant EBITDA improvement in manufacturing or distribution businesses.

**Headcount optimisation** — eliminating redundant roles (particularly after bolt-on integration), outsourcing non-core functions, and right-sizing management layers. This is often the most sensitive area and requires careful execution to avoid losing critical talent or damaging morale.

**Facility rationalisation** — consolidating production sites, warehouses, or offices to reduce fixed costs. Particularly relevant after bolt-on acquisitions create overlapping geographic footprints.

**Overhead reduction** — reducing corporate overhead (professional fees, travel, subscriptions, IT costs) to align with private company expectations rather than public company standards. Former public companies often carry significant compliance and governance costs that can be reduced under private ownership.

**Operational efficiency** — lean manufacturing, process automation, and waste reduction. PE operating partners with manufacturing expertise can identify substantial efficiency gains in production-heavy businesses.

### Margin Expansion Through Mix Improvement

Beyond pure cost reduction, PE firms improve margins by shifting the revenue mix toward higher-margin products, services, or customer segments:

- Transitioning from low-margin hardware sales to high-margin recurring service contracts
- Exiting unprofitable customer relationships or product lines
- Shifting from direct sales to franchise or licensing models (the "asset-light" transformation)
