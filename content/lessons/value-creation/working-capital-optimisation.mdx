Working capital optimisation is a reliable value creation lever because it releases cash that can be used for debt repayment, reducing leverage and increasing equity value.

### Accounts Receivable Optimisation

- Tighten credit terms (move from 60-day to 30-day payment terms where market conditions allow)
- Improve invoicing processes (invoice on delivery, not at month-end; automate invoice generation)
- Implement early payment discounts (2/10 net 30: 2% discount if paid within 10 days)
- Assign dedicated credit control resources to large overdue accounts
- Implement automated reminders and escalation procedures

### Inventory Optimisation

- Improve demand forecasting to reduce safety stock levels
- Implement ABC analysis (classify inventory by value and turnover rate; focus management attention on the high-value, slow-moving items)
- Rationalise SKU count — eliminate slow-selling or unprofitable product lines
- Negotiate vendor-managed inventory (VMI) or consignment arrangements with key suppliers
- Implement just-in-time (JIT) practices where supply chain reliability allows

### Accounts Payable Optimisation

- Negotiate longer payment terms with suppliers (move from 30 days to 45–60 days)
- Centralise procurement to leverage volume and negotiate better terms
- Implement purchase-to-pay automation to eliminate manual processing delays
- Use supply chain finance programmes (reverse factoring) to extend payment terms without damaging supplier relationships

### Quantifying the Cash Impact

For a company with €200M revenue:
- Reducing DSO by 5 days: €200M / 365 x 5 = **€2.7M cash released**
- Reducing DIO by 5 days: (assuming €120M COGS) €120M / 365 x 5 = **€1.6M cash released**
- Extending DPO by 5 days: €120M / 365 x 5 = **€1.6M cash released**
- Total: approximately **€6M** in one-time cash release — equivalent to accelerating debt paydown and directly increasing equity value.
