PE-backed companies operate under a governance framework that is fundamentally different from both public companies and privately held family businesses.

### Board Composition and Cadence

PE-backed boards typically include:
- 2–3 PE firm representatives (deal team partner, principal, and/or operating partner)
- The CEO and sometimes the CFO
- 1–2 independent directors (industry experts, former executives, or functional specialists)

Board meetings occur monthly (not quarterly as in most public companies), with detailed financial and operational reporting packages distributed in advance. This monthly cadence provides the PE firm with real-time visibility into performance and enables faster course correction.

### Reporting Standards

PE-backed companies are expected to produce:
- Monthly management accounts (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow) within 10–15 business days of month-end
- Monthly KPI dashboards tracking operational and commercial metrics
- Quarterly board packs with strategic updates, pipeline reviews, and risk assessments
- Annual budgets and rolling forecasts updated quarterly
- Covenant compliance reporting (leverage ratio, interest coverage)

Many mid-market companies acquired by PE do not have this reporting capability at the time of acquisition. Building it is often a first-100-days priority.

### Decision Rights and Approval Thresholds

The shareholder agreement between the PE fund and management typically specifies decision rights — which actions management can take autonomously and which require board or shareholder approval:

- **Management authority:** Day-to-day operations, hiring below a threshold, contracts below a specified value
- **Board approval required:** Capital expenditure above a threshold, new market entry, management hires above a level, changes to strategy
- **Shareholder (PE fund) approval required:** Acquisitions, significant debt changes, capital structure modifications, exit decisions

This framework balances operational agility with investor oversight.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Supplement

- **Value-based pricing** — pricing based on the value delivered to the customer rather than cost-plus
- **ABC analysis** — classifying inventory by value and turnover to focus management attention
- **Value stream mapping** — lean methodology for identifying value-adding and non-value-adding process steps
- **Kaizen** — the practice of continuous, incremental operational improvement
- **Decision rights** — the governance framework specifying which decisions require board or shareholder approval
