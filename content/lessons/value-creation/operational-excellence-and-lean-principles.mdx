PE firms increasingly apply structured operational improvement methodologies — particularly lean manufacturing and Six Sigma — to portfolio companies, especially in manufacturing, distribution, and services.

### Lean Principles in PE Context

**Value stream mapping.** Map the entire process from customer order to delivery, identifying every step that adds value and every step that does not (waste). In a typical manufacturing operation, only 5–15% of process steps add value from the customer's perspective.

**Waste elimination.** The seven wastes (TIMWOOD): Transport, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Over-production, Over-processing, Defects. Each waste category represents potential cost reduction.

**Continuous improvement (Kaizen).** Embedding a culture of ongoing, incremental improvement. This is harder to implement than a one-time cost reduction programme, but more sustainable.

### How PE Firms Apply Operational Excellence

**Diagnostic phase (first 60–90 days).** Operating partners or external consultants assess the portfolio company's operations, benchmark against best-in-class, and identify the highest-impact improvement opportunities.

**Implementation phase (months 3–18).** Deploy improvement projects with clear ownership, KPIs, and milestones. Typical initiatives: production line rebalancing, setup time reduction (SMED), quality improvement, warehouse layout optimisation, transport route optimisation.

**Sustaining phase (ongoing).** Embed continuous improvement in the operating culture through training, performance management, and regular management review of operational KPIs.

### Case Illustration: Operational Improvement in Industrial PE

PE firms specialising in industrial buyouts (Clayton Dubilier & Rice, Danaher's former PE operations, and mid-market industrial specialists) have built repeatable operational improvement playbooks. The Danaher Business System (DBS) — a lean management system originally inspired by the Toyota Production System — became the template for how PE-owned industrial companies drive operational excellence. Although Danaher is now a public company, its approach has been widely adopted by PE firms managing manufacturing and distribution portfolios.
