PE-backed digital transformation is not about technology for its own sake. It is about deploying technology to drive measurable EBITDA improvement — whether through revenue enablement, cost reduction, or data-driven decision-making.

### Common Digital Initiatives

**ERP implementation or upgrade** — many mid-market companies operate on outdated or fragmented systems. A modern ERP (enterprise resource planning) system improves financial reporting, operational visibility, and decision-making speed. This is particularly critical for buy-and-build platforms integrating multiple acquired companies.

**E-commerce and digital sales** — adding or improving digital channels to reach new customers, reduce cost-to-serve, and capture transaction data.

**Data analytics and business intelligence** — building dashboards and analytics capabilities that allow management to track KPIs in real time, identify trends, and make data-driven decisions.

**Automation** — robotic process automation (RPA) for back-office tasks (invoice processing, reporting, compliance) and manufacturing automation to reduce labour costs and improve quality.

**Cybersecurity** — increasingly a diligence item and a post-acquisition investment priority, particularly for businesses handling sensitive data or operating in regulated industries.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **100-day plan** — a structured action plan for the first 100 days after acquisition, translating the investment thesis into prioritised initiatives
- **Buy-and-build** — a PE strategy of acquiring a platform company and completing bolt-on acquisitions to build scale
- **Multiple arbitrage** — the value created by acquiring bolt-on businesses at lower multiples than the platform's exit multiple
- **Management equity programme (MEP)** — an equity incentive plan aligning management's financial interests with the PE fund's return objectives
- **Sweet equity** — a mechanism giving management a disproportionate share of equity upside above a defined performance threshold
