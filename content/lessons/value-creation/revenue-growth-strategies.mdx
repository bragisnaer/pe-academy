Revenue growth is the most powerful value creation lever in PE because it drives both EBITDA expansion (if margins hold) and multiple expansion at exit (larger, faster-growing businesses command higher multiples).

### Organic Growth Levers

**Pricing optimisation** — many mid-market companies have never conducted systematic pricing analysis. Common opportunities include: eliminating unprofitable discounts, implementing annual price increases tied to inflation, moving to value-based pricing (charging based on value delivered rather than cost-plus), and rationalising price lists that have accumulated inconsistencies over years.

**Sales force effectiveness** — investing in sales capacity (hiring additional salespeople), improving sales processes (CRM implementation, pipeline management), and aligning incentive structures to drive growth. A company that has relied on inbound demand or founder relationships often has significant upside from building a professional outbound sales organisation.

**Geographic expansion** — entering new regions, whether domestically or internationally. For European businesses, this often means expanding from a home market (e.g., Germany) into adjacent markets (Nordics, Benelux, France). For US businesses, international expansion or entry into underpenetrated domestic regions.

**Product and service expansion** — adjacent product lines, new service tiers, or cross-selling to existing customers. The lowest-risk growth strategy is selling more to existing satisfied customers.

**Channel development** — adding e-commerce capability, launching partner programmes, or developing distributor networks in new markets.

### Inorganic Growth (Buy-and-Build)

**Buy-and-build** (also called a platform strategy) is one of the most common PE value creation approaches. The PE firm acquires a "platform" company, then makes smaller "bolt-on" acquisitions to build scale.

Buy-and-build works because of **multiple arbitrage**: the platform company might be acquired at 10x EBITDA, while smaller bolt-on targets are acquired at 5–7x EBITDA. By integrating the bolt-ons into the platform, the combined entity is valued at the higher platform multiple at exit — creating value purely through consolidation.

**Case illustration: Addtech (Nordic industrial distribution)** — While not a PE case, Addtech's serial acquisition model in the Nordic industrial sector illustrates the buy-and-build logic. The company has completed over 100 acquisitions of small niche distributors, integrating them into a scalable platform while retaining entrepreneurial management. PE firms like Lifco (Wallenberg-controlled, publicly listed) and Indutrade follow similar models. The strategy works in fragmented markets where small operators lack scale advantages that a platform can provide.
