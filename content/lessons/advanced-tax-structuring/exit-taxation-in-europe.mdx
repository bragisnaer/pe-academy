> **Market-specific: EU/EEA**

### Participation Exemption

Most European jurisdictions offer a **participation exemption** — an exemption from capital gains tax on the disposal of qualifying subsidiary shares. This allows the PE fund to sell its investment without incurring capital gains tax at the holding company level, provided the conditions are met.

**Denmark:** Capital gains on shares held by a Danish company for more than one year in subsidiaries (where ownership exceeds 10%) are generally exempt from tax under the participation exemption (Selskabsskatteloven § 13). Gains on portfolio shares (below 10% ownership) are taxable.

**Luxembourg:** The participation exemption applies to gains on disposal of qualifying subsidiaries (10% ownership or acquisition cost above €1.2 million, held for at least 12 months).

**Netherlands:** The participation exemption applies to gains on disposal of subsidiaries where the holding exceeds 5%.

**UK:** The Substantial Shareholding Exemption (SSE) exempts gains on disposal of subsidiaries (10%+ holding, held for at least 12 months, both companies must be trading companies or members of a trading group).

### Withholding Taxes on Distributions

When a portfolio company pays dividends to its holding company, withholding tax may apply. The rate depends on the countries involved and applicable tax treaties.

**Intra-EU:** The EU Parent-Subsidiary Directive generally eliminates withholding tax on dividends between EU parent companies and their EU subsidiaries (subject to anti-abuse provisions and minimum holding requirements).

**Denmark:** No withholding tax on dividends paid by a Danish subsidiary to an EU/EEA parent company that holds 10%+ of the shares, provided anti-abuse conditions are met.

### OECD Pillar Two: Global Minimum Tax

The OECD/G20 Inclusive Framework on Base Erosion and Profit Shifting introduced Pillar Two — a global minimum effective tax rate of 15% for multinational groups with consolidated revenue above €750 million. Pillar Two has been implemented across the EU (including Denmark) through the EU Minimum Tax Directive, effective from 2024.

**Impact on PE:** Portfolio companies within in-scope groups must pay an effective tax rate of at least 15% in each jurisdiction. If a subsidiary's effective rate is below 15%, a "top-up tax" is levied to bring it to 15%. This constrains the use of low-tax jurisdictions in PE holding structures.
