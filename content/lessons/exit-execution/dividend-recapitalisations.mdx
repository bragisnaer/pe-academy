A **dividend recapitalisation** (dividend recap) is not a full exit — it is a partial return of capital to the PE fund during the hold period. The portfolio company refinances its debt (or takes on additional debt) and uses the proceeds to pay a special dividend to the equity holders (the PE fund).

### How It Works

The portfolio company's EBITDA has grown since acquisition, creating additional debt capacity. The company raises new debt against this higher EBITDA base and distributes the proceeds to the PE fund.

**Example:** A company was acquired at 5x leverage (debt/EBITDA) with €50M EBITDA and €250M debt. After three years, EBITDA has grown to €70M and debt has been partially repaid to €200M. Current leverage is 2.9x. The company raises €145M in new debt (bringing leverage to 4.9x) and distributes €145M to the PE fund as a dividend.

### Strategic Value

Dividend recaps serve several purposes for the PE fund:

- **De-risking** — by returning a portion of equity early, the GP reduces the capital at risk. Even if the eventual exit disappoints, the fund has already recovered a significant share of its investment.
- **IRR enhancement** — returning cash earlier improves the fund's IRR (because IRR is time-sensitive).
- **LP distributions** — generating DPI (cash distributed to LPs) earlier in the fund life, which improves the fund's track record for the next fundraise.

### Controversies

Dividend recaps are controversial. Critics argue they prioritise GP returns over portfolio company financial health — loading a company with debt to pay its owners. If the business subsequently deteriorates, the additional debt can tip it into distress.

Lenders have become more cautious about funding dividend recaps, and some LPAs restrict or require LPAC approval for dividend recaps above certain leverage thresholds.
