Not every exit is a full sale. PE firms increasingly use partial exit structures to de-risk, return capital to LPs, and retain upside.

### Types of Partial Exits

**Minority stake sale** — the PE firm sells a minority stake (10–30%) to a new investor (often a sovereign wealth fund, family office, or secondary fund) while retaining majority control. This provides liquidity and a valuation benchmark while preserving the PE firm's ability to continue executing the value creation plan.

**IPO with retained stake** — the PE firm lists the company but retains a majority position, selling down over 1–3 years through secondary offerings. Covered in the IPO lesson.

**Recapitalisation with partial equity take-out** — the company refinances its debt (or raises new debt) and distributes proceeds to the PE fund. The PE firm retains ownership but has de-risked by recovering a portion of equity.

### Strategic Logic

Partial exits allow the PE firm to:
- Return capital to LPs (improving DPI and the fund's track record for the next fundraise)
- De-risk by reducing capital at risk
- Retain exposure to ongoing value creation
- Achieve an interim valuation reference point

### Key Terms Introduced in This Supplement

- **Vendor due diligence (VDD)** — DD commissioned by the seller before a sale process to identify and resolve issues proactively
- **Dual-track process** — running an M&A and IPO process simultaneously to maximise optionality
- **Bid-ask spread** — the gap between the seller's price expectation and the buyer's offer
- **Earn-out** — contingent consideration dependent on post-closing performance milestones
- **Partial exit** — selling a portion of the PE firm's stake while retaining ongoing ownership
