Exit timing is one of the most consequential decisions a PE firm makes. The same business sold at different points in the cycle can produce dramatically different returns.

### Factors Influencing Exit Timing

**Value creation completion** — has the value creation plan been substantially executed? Selling too early leaves value on the table; selling too late risks the market turning.

**Market conditions** — are credit markets open (enabling financial buyers to pay competitive prices)? Are public markets strong (supporting IPO valuations)? Are strategic buyers active?

**Sector multiples** — are sector valuations at a cyclical peak, trough, or mid-range? Selling a healthcare services business at the peak of healthcare multiples can add 1–2x EBITDA turns to the exit price.

**Fund lifecycle** — is the fund approaching the end of its investment period or overall fund life? Funds nearing expiration may need to exit regardless of market conditions.

**LP expectations** — are LPs pressing for distributions? Has the GP communicated realistic exit timing expectations?

### The Exit Paradox

The best time to sell is often when the business is performing well and the market is strong — which is also when the temptation to hold is strongest. Disciplined exit execution requires setting clear exit criteria in advance and acting when those criteria are met, rather than waiting for perfection.

### Key Terms Introduced in This Module

- **Trade sale** — sale of a portfolio company to a strategic corporate buyer
- **Secondary buyout (SBO)** — sale of a portfolio company from one PE firm to another
- **Continuation vehicle** — a GP-led secondary that extends the hold period by moving a portfolio company into a new fund vehicle
- **IPO** — initial public offering; listing a portfolio company on a public stock exchange
- **Dividend recapitalisation** — refinancing a portfolio company's debt and distributing the proceeds to the PE fund as a dividend
- **Lock-up period** — the contractual restriction preventing PE firms from selling shares immediately after an IPO
