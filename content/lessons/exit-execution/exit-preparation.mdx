The best PE firms begin preparing for exit from the day they acquire a company. Exit preparation is not about cosmetic improvements — it is about building a business that a buyer will pay a premium for because it is clean, well-documented, and demonstrably performing.

### The "Exit-Ready" Checklist

**Financial readiness:**
- Audited financial statements (ideally under IFRS or US GAAP if targeting international buyers)
- Clean EBITDA with minimal add-backs (each add-back a buyer must evaluate is a source of friction and potential price reduction)
- Demonstrated FCF conversion above 60%
- Working capital normalised and stable
- No off-balance-sheet surprises

**Operational readiness:**
- Management team that can operate independently (not dependent on PE firm involvement)
- Robust management reporting (monthly accounts within 10 days, KPI dashboards, rolling forecasts)
- Documented processes and systems (not dependent on individual knowledge)
- Clean corporate structure (simplified legal entities, no dormant subsidiaries, intercompany balances eliminated)

**Commercial readiness:**
- Diversified customer base (no single customer above 15% of revenue at exit)
- Growing pipeline with measurable win rates
- Multi-year contracts or demonstrated retention rates
- Clear growth story supported by data

**Legal readiness:**
- No pending litigation or regulatory issues
- All IP properly registered and owned
- Contracts reviewed for change-of-control issues (resolved before the sale process, not during)
- Employment matters clean (no outstanding disputes, key employees locked in)

### Vendor Due Diligence (VDD)

Many PE firms commission **vendor due diligence** — a financial, commercial, and sometimes legal/tax DD report prepared on behalf of the seller before the sale process begins. VDD serves several purposes:

- Identifies and resolves issues before buyers discover them (no surprises in the data room)
- Speeds the sale process (buyers can rely on the VDD report and conduct confirmatory diligence only)
- Provides a "single version of truth" for adjusted EBITDA, normalised working capital, and net debt
- Demonstrates professionalism and transparency, building buyer confidence

VDD is standard practice in European PE exits and increasingly common in the US.
